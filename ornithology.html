Natural History and Ornithology

Cranes are tall, bipedal creatures that can live up to forty years. Cranes are in the family Gruidae and are organized into five separate Genuses: Balearic, Anthropoides, Bugeranus, Leucogeranus, and Antigone. Most cranes inhabit wet areas, yet others utilize vast open areas. Cranes will eat just about anything: leaves, nuts, berries, insects, and mammals, snakes, lizards, and other birds. Cranes form pair bonds for life, and will engage in elaborate courtship dances to reinforce these bonds. Many modern and ancient people have been amazed by these dances; some cultures have even incorporated the dance of the crane into their own rituals. Cranes typically lay 2 eggs, sometimes more, and both parents participate in nest construction, incubation, and raising the chicks.
