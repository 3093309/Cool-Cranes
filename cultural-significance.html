Cultural Significance

Cranes are revered by many cultures. In Japan, the crane, specifically the Red-crowned Crane, is a symbol of happiness and longevity; fables say it lives for a thousand years. Common Crane remains are found throughout the Mediterrainan. It is suspected that Neolithic peoples implemented the dance of the crane into their own dances. Some Native American tribes considered seeing a crane while fishing good luck; cranes where also seen as a symbol of peace and leadership. The Cheyenne tribe associated Sandhill Cranes with lightning.
